About Me

How I transitioned into programming

In college, I studied mathematics due to its elegance and inherent problem-solving. After college, I got my Masters in Actuarial Science, and ended up work at a commercial insurer . As an actuary, I spent a lot of time (in hindsight more time than I would have liked) with Excel and SQL. My favorite moments at my job were using coding up VBA scripts to automate away tedious work. Yes! VBA was my gateway drug into programming. What I especially loved about programming were the time spent tinkering around in the editor; the instant sense of gratification from seeing whether your code worked; and the problem-solving. Slowly I started wishing more and more that I could just code full-time, but I was too scared to make the leap. While I was going through this internal conflict, the insurance industry and my employer was going through some serious issues. Things got so bad that they had to lay-off a number of employees, which included me. Although shocking, this event pushed me to transition into tech. However, how was I going to get good enough to actually get a tech job?

My Coding Bootcamp Search

I vaguley knew about coding bootcamps from news about a bunch of classmates that had successfully completed ones to transition to tech! Looking more into them, I found out that coding bootcamps were typically structured full-time programs that last around 3 months, and taught students how to code from the bottom up. Many of their graduates learn enough to be competitive with graduates from major CS programs. The bootcamp modeled appealed to me because not only was it structured and highly focused but it was focused on practical material relevant to a professional developer.

I immediately started researching into the best coding bootcamps in the US. I looked at bootcamps in Chicago (where I lived), NYC, and SF. Simultaneously I taught myself Ruby during this time to prepare for my upcoming technical interviews. After getting accepted into five bootcamps, I decided to go to Fullstack Academy, the best coding bootcamp in Chicago. What drew me to Fullstack Academy were the quality of its instructors, the low teacher-to-student ratio , and its selectivity . I also would not have to deal with the stress of moving and familiarizing myself to a new place.

Fullstack Academy

Going to Fullstack was like drinking water from a firehose. It was an overwhelming but exciting experience, which I intend to write more about in the future. THe main thing that I loved about Fullstack was how it taught us how to teach ourselves to become better developers. We did not mindlessly learn how to use libraries or frameworks; instead we would delve into the foundations of how things worked. For example, Bootstrap is a popular modern CSS framework that a lot of people can use without ever understanding the details. In one of our workshops, Shoestring, we had to build our own Bootstrap-like CSS framework. We also had workshops dealing with CS fundamentals like Algorithms and Data Structures. Learning all this material was made much easier by our amazing instructors, Nick Drane and Zeke Nierenberg, and awesome classmates/partners. After finishing my group capstone project HelloCodeJS , I was confident in my abilities as a developer.
